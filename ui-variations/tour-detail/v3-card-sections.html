Overview

The Machame Route, known as the "Whiskey Route," is one of the most scenic and popular paths to Uhuru Peak. This route offers exceptional views and diverse landscapes, from lush rainforest to alpine desert. The longer duration allows for better acclimatization, contributing to our 92% success rate.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

1

Machame Gate to Machame Camp

1,800m to 3,000m 5-6 hours 11km trek

Your adventure begins with a morning drive from Moshi to Machame Gate (1,800m). After registration and final preparations, enter the pristine montane rainforest. The trail winds through a canopy of ancient trees draped with hanging moss, alive with birdsong and the calls of Colobus monkeys. Arrive at Machame Camp as the forest thins, with views opening up toward the mountain.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to climb?
The best times are January-March and June-October, which are the dry seasons. July-August offers the clearest skies, while January-February has fewer crowds.
How fit do I need to be?
A good level of fitness is required. We recommend training 3-4 months before your climb with hiking, cardio, and strength exercises. Previous high-altitude experience helps but isn't essential.
What about altitude sickness?
Our guides are trained to recognize and manage altitude sickness. The 7-day itinerary allows proper acclimatization. We follow a "climb high, sleep low" approach and monitor all climbers regularly.
